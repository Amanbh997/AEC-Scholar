Develop a research proposal for: **$ARGUMENTS**

Use the **research-strategist** and **methodologist** agents, plus `research-methods`, `aec-domains`,
`systematic-review` and `research-ethics-integrity`. (For *funding* bids use `/aec-scholar:grant-proposal`.)

Produce a structured proposal the user can refine:

1. **Background & significance** — the problem, why it matters (scholarly + industry), and the conceptual
   framing. Ground terminology in `aec-domains`.

2. **Gap & rationale** — the specific, defensible gap (link `/aec-scholar:research-gap`); justify why it is
   worth a multi-year study and tell the user to confirm it with a real search.

3. **Aim, research questions & objectives** — a clear central aim and 1–4 well-formed RQs (PICO/PCC/SPIDER;
   FINER-checked — see `/aec-scholar:research-question`).

4. **Proposed methodology** — design, data/sites, sampling, methods per RQ, analysis, validity, and ethics
   (link `/aec-scholar:methodology`). Be realistic about access and scale.

5. **Expected contribution & impact** — what new knowledge/artifact results, and academic + practical
   implications.

6. **Work plan & timeline** — phases, milestones, deliverables, and a Gantt outline; risks and mitigations.

7. **Preliminary work** (if any) and **resources** needed.

Mark every placeholder the researcher must supply with real evidence (preliminary data, supervisor input,
access agreements). Tailor to a confirmation/upgrade report, a PhD application, or a project proposal as the
user indicates.
