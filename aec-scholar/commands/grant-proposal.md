Help develop a grant proposal for: **$ARGUMENTS**

Use the **methodologist**, **academic-writer** and **aec-domain-expert** agents, plus `research-methods`,
`aec-domains` and `research-ethics-integrity`.

1. **Frame the case.** Sharpen the problem, its significance (scientific + societal/industry — for AEC, tie
   to decarbonization, productivity, safety, housing, resilience as relevant), and the **gap** the project
   fills. Articulate the central research question and an ambitious-but-credible aim.

2. **Objectives & work plan.** Define specific objectives/work packages with logical flow, milestones,
   deliverables and a timeline (offer a Gantt outline). Map methods to objectives (link `research-methods`).

3. **Novelty & impact.** State what's genuinely new and the pathways to impact (academic outputs, industry
   uptake, policy, standards, training). Include a dissemination & open-science plan.

4. **Feasibility & risk.** Team capability, resources/equipment/data access, and a risk register with
   mitigations. Demonstrate the work is achievable in scope and budget.

5. **Compliance.** Address ethics/data protection, a Data Management Plan (FAIR), and any funder-specific
   requirements (if the funder/scheme is named, tailor to its structure — and tell the user to verify the
   current call text and criteria on the funder's site).

6. **Budget logic** — outline justifiable cost categories (staff, equipment, travel, dissemination, data)
   without inventing figures.

Produce a structured draft the user can expand, and a checklist of what they must supply (preliminary data,
CVs, letters of support, exact budget). Tailor language to the funder's evaluation criteria where known.

**Integrity:** don't fabricate preliminary results, partners, or track record. Mark every placeholder the
applicant must fill with real evidence.
