Optimize the title and keywords for: **$ARGUMENTS**

Use the **academic-writer** and **aec-domain-expert** agents, with `aec-domains` (terminology) and
`aec-journals` (indexing/SEO conventions).

1. **Titles.** Propose 4–6 candidate titles spanning styles (descriptive, method-forward, finding-forward,
   and a question form). Each should be specific, accurate, keyword-rich at the front, free of undefined
   acronyms, and non-over-claiming. Briefly note the trade-off of each, then recommend one.

2. **Keywords.** Propose 5–8 keywords for discoverability:
   - mix exact-topic terms with broader indexing terms,
   - include canonical AEC terminology and common synonyms searchers actually use (from `aec-domains`),
   - avoid words already in the title where the venue discourages repetition (check the venue's rule),
   - prefer terms that match database thesauri/indexing.

3. **Discoverability check.** Note how the title+keywords position the paper for the target audience and
   search behavior, and whether they align with the likely venue's norms.

Keep everything truthful to the actual contribution — a catchy title that over-promises will be punished in
review and erode trust. Recommend the final title + keyword set explicitly.
