Turn this research idea into well-formed research questions: **$ARGUMENTS**

Use the **methodologist** agent and the `research-methods` skill.

Deliver:

1. **Aim classification.** Is the study exploratory, descriptive, explanatory, predictive, or
   design-and-evaluate? This determines question form.

2. **Framed questions.** Draft 1–3 primary research questions (plus sub-questions) using the appropriate
   framework:
   - PICO/PICOC for intervention/effect studies,
   - PCC for scoping work,
   - SPIDER for qualitative studies.
   Make each question specific, answerable, and scoped (not "how can BIM improve construction?").

3. **Objectives & hypotheses.** Convert questions into concrete objectives, and — for explanatory/predictive
   work — testable hypotheses with named variables (IV/DV/controls).

4. **FINER check.** Evaluate each question for Feasible, Interesting, Novel, Ethical, Relevant — and flag any
   that fail (e.g., not feasible with available data/access).

5. **Method signpost.** For each question, indicate the likely method family and what data it requires, then
   offer to proceed to `/aec-scholar:methodology`.

Keep questions tightly aligned to the stated gap and realistic for an AEC study's typical access and scale.
