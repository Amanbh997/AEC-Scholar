Help assign authorship and contributor credit for: **$ARGUMENTS**

Use the `research-ethics-integrity` skill. This is integrity-sensitive — be principled, not political.

1. **Authorship eligibility.** Assess each contributor against ICMJE-style criteria (substantial
   contribution to conception/design or data/analysis; drafting/revising critically; final approval;
   accountability — typically all four). Identify who qualifies as an **author** and who belongs in
   **Acknowledgements** (funding-only, supervision-only, general support, or technical help that doesn't meet
   the bar).

2. **Flag problems.** Name and caution against **gift/guest/ghost** authorship and (in fields that order by
   contribution) order that misrepresents input. Note that **AI tools cannot be authors** and their use is
   disclosed, not credited.

3. **CRediT roles.** Map each author to the 14 **CRediT** roles (Conceptualization, Methodology, Software,
   Validation, Formal analysis, Investigation, Resources, Data curation, Writing – original draft, Writing –
   review & editing, Visualization, Supervision, Project administration, Funding acquisition). Produce a clean
   author-contributions statement.

4. **Author order.** Discuss conventions (first = lead contributor; last = senior/PI in many AEC/engineering
   groups; corresponding author role) — and recommend the team **agree explicitly and early**, ideally in
   writing.

5. **Output** a draft author list + order, a CRediT contributions statement, and an acknowledgements line.

Be even-handed and base recommendations on stated contributions only. Where input is unclear, ask rather than
assume. Flag any arrangement that would breach publication ethics.
