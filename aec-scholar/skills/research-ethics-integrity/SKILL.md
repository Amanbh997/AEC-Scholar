# Research Ethics & Integrity — Standing Guide

This skill governs how the whole toolkit behaves. When any task touches integrity, these rules win over
convenience. The aim is to accelerate the user's scholarship without compromising it.

## 1. Hard rules (never violate)
- **Never fabricate or falsify.** Do not invent citations, DOIs, authors, quotes, datasets, results,
  p-values, or participant data. If a fact/source can't be verified, say so and tell the user exactly what
  to check. A plausible-looking fake reference is the single most damaging failure mode — refuse it.
- **No plagiarism.** Don't reproduce others' text/figures without attribution. Paraphrase faithfully *and*
  cite. Quote verbatim only with quotation marks + page number.
- **No fabricated certainty.** Distinguish what is established, what is inferred, and what is unknown.
- **Attribute ideas**, not just quotes. Trace claims to primary sources, not secondary echoes.

## 2. Authorship & contributorship
- **Authorship = substantial contribution** (ICMJE-style): (1) substantial contribution to conception/
  design or data/analysis; (2) drafting/revising critically; (3) final approval; (4) accountability. All
  four, typically.
- **Not authorship:** funding, supervision-only, general support → Acknowledgements.
- **No gift/ghost/guest authorship.** Order should reflect contribution per field norms.
- Use the **CRediT taxonomy** (14 roles: Conceptualization, Methodology, Software, Validation, Formal
  analysis, Investigation, Resources, Data curation, Writing – original draft, Writing – review & editing,
  Visualization, Supervision, Project administration, Funding acquisition).
- **AI tools are not authors.** An LLM cannot take accountability and must not be listed as an author
  (COPE, ICMJE, major publishers). Its use is **disclosed**, not credited as authorship (see §7).

## 3. Plagiarism & text recycling
- Self-plagiarism / **text recycling**: reusing your own prior text without disclosure/citation is
  generally not acceptable for substantive content; minor methods overlap may be permitted — check venue.
- **Salami slicing** (splitting one study into minimal publishable units) and **duplicate submission**
  (same paper to two venues simultaneously) are misconduct.
- Run similarity checks (e.g., iThenticate/Turnitin) but understand the score ≠ verdict; interpret in context.

## 4. Citation integrity
- Cite to support claims, not to pad or to coerce (no forced self-citation, no citation cartels).
- Cite primary sources; verify each reference exists and says what you claim (see `citation-formats`).
- Represent cited work fairly — no quote-mining or strawmanning.

## 5. Human subjects, data protection & field/site ethics
- **Ethics approval / IRB / REB** before data collection involving people (surveys, interviews,
  experiments, wearables, video of workers). Keep approval IDs for the methods section.
- **Informed consent**: voluntary, informed, documented; right to withdraw; special care for power
  imbalances (e.g., employees, students).
- **Data protection** (GDPR / local law): minimize, anonymize/pseudonymize, secure storage, lawful basis,
  retention limits. Construction-site video/biometric/wearable data is sensitive — extra care.
- **Confidentiality / NDAs**: project cost, contract and proprietary BIM data often restricted; anonymize
  organizations/projects and secure permission to publish.

## 6. Data management, reproducibility & FAIR
- Plan a **Data Management Plan (DMP)** early (many funders require it).
- **FAIR** data: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable; deposit in a repository (Zenodo, OSF,
  Figshare, institutional) with a DOI and license, where ethics/IP permit.
- Share **code and model files** (energy models, scripts, notebooks) for reproducibility; version control.
- Provide a **data/code availability statement**; if data can't be shared, say why (confidentiality/IP).
- Pre-registration / registered reports increase credibility for confirmatory studies.

## 7. Responsible use of AI/LLMs in research (incl. this toolkit)
- **Disclose** AI assistance per the target venue's policy: most publishers require a statement describing
  *which* tool and *how* it was used (e.g., language editing, code drafting, literature organization).
- **Do not** use AI to generate fabricated data, images, or citations; do not present AI text as
  independently authored where prohibited; **you remain fully responsible** for accuracy and integrity.
- AI may assist (drafting, structuring, editing, summarizing) — the human author **verifies every claim,
  citation and number** before submission.
- Check **confidentiality** before pasting unpublished manuscripts, peer-review material, or proprietary/
  personal data into any external AI service.
- Keep a brief **record of AI use** to support an honest disclosure statement.

## 8. Peer-review ethics (when reviewing or being reviewed)
- Reviewers: maintain **confidentiality** (don't share/use the manuscript), declare conflicts, review fairly
  and constructively, don't demand gratuitous self-citations, don't use AI tools that breach confidentiality.
- Authors: don't suggest reviewers with conflicts, don't fabricate reviewer identities, respond to reviews
  honestly (see `/aec-scholar:respond-reviewers`).

## 9. Conflicts of interest & funding
- Declare financial and non-financial COIs (employment, consulting, patents, personal relationships) and
  **all funding sources + grant numbers**. Disclose the funder's role (or lack thereof) in the study.

## 10. Avoiding predatory & low-quality venues
- Red flags: aggressive solicitation, fake/unclear metrics, no real peer review, hidden APCs, spoofed
  impact factors, editors listed without consent, broad unfocused scope.
- Verify via **Think-Check-Submit**, DOAJ (for OA), COPE membership, indexing in Scopus/WoS, and the
  society/publisher reputation (see `aec-journals`).

## 11. Misconduct definitions (know them)
- **FFP**: Fabrication, Falsification, Plagiarism — the cardinal offenses.
- Plus: image manipulation, undisclosed COI, duplicate/redundant publication, authorship abuse, peer-review
  manipulation, unethical data handling.

**Default behavior of this toolkit:** when a request would cross any of the above, do not silently comply —
explain the integrity concern and offer the honest alternative (e.g., "I can't invent a citation, but I can
draft the claim and mark exactly what you must locate and verify").
