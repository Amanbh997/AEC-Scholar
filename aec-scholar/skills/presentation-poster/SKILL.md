# Conference Presentations & Posters — Craft Guide

Help researchers communicate work clearly to a live audience. The medium is not a paper — it is a story with
one memorable takeaway.

## 1. Oral presentations
**Structure (story, not IMRaD read aloud):**
1. **Hook / motivation** — the real-world problem in one slide (why care?).
2. **Gap & question** — what's missing; your specific question.
3. **Approach** — method at the right altitude (audience-appropriate; don't drown in detail).
4. **Key results** — 1–3 findings, each on its own clean slide, with the figure doing the work.
5. **So what** — contribution, implications (academic + industry), limitations honestly, next steps.
6. **Takeaway slide** — one sentence you want remembered + contact/QR to paper.

**Slide design:**
- One idea per slide; **few words, large font** (≥24 pt body, ≥28–32 pt headers). Headlines that state the
  point ("Detection F1 improved 0.74→0.88"), not labels ("Results").
- Figures > text; reuse the paper's figures but **simplify** for projection (bigger fonts, fewer series,
  colour-blind-safe — see `scientific-figures`). High contrast; avoid dense tables and equations dumps.
- Progressive reveal for complex builds; minimal animation.

**Timing & delivery:**
- ~1 slide/minute as a rough cap; rehearse to the **exact** slot (e.g., 12 + 3 min Q&A at ISARC/CRC). Never
  overrun. Have a hard "if short on time, skip to slide X" plan.
- Practice aloud, standing; know your opening and closing cold. Signpost ("First… now the key result…").
- Face the audience, not the screen; use a pointer sparingly.

**Q&A:**
- Anticipate the obvious questions (validation, generalizability, scalability, comparison to baseline,
  practical adoption). Prepare honest answers and a few **backup slides** (extra data, methods detail).
- Repeat/clarify the question; answer concisely; "I don't know — good question, here's how I'd find out" is
  a strong answer. Don't bluff.

## 2. Posters
**Goal:** readable from ~1.5 m, understood in <1 minute, with you adding depth in conversation.

**Modern "better poster" format (recommended):**
- A large central panel stating the **single main finding in plain language** (the "billboard"), with
  supporting method/results/implications in side columns and a small "ammo bar" of detail.
- Minimal text, generous white space, one hero figure.

**Classic format** (if required): Title/authors → Introduction & gap → Methods → Results (figure-led) →
Conclusions → key references + QR to paper/data.

**Design rules:**
- Clear visual hierarchy and reading order (columns, numbered sections). Big title; legible body (~24–32 pt
  equivalent at print size). Colour-blind-safe, consistent palette. Vector graphics; check at full print
  scale. Include a **QR code** to the paper/preprint/data and your contact/ORCID.
- One takeaway sentence, prominent. Cut everything that isn't needed to land it.

## 3. Lightning / 3-minute talks
- One problem, one finding, one slide if mandated. Memorize it. End with the takeaway + where to find more.

## 4. Graphical / visual abstracts
- A single self-explanatory figure of the core idea/finding (see `scientific-figures`). Reuse for the poster
  billboard and the takeaway slide for a consistent identity.

## 5. Integrity
- Present only real results; don't overstate findings for a live audience any more than in print. Acknowledge
  collaborators and funding. Disclose limitations honestly (`research-ethics-integrity`).
