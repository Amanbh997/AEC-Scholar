# AEC Journals, Conferences, Databases & Metrics — Reference

Use this to match a manuscript to credible venues, build a defensible source list for a review, and
interpret bibliometric metrics correctly. **Always tell the user to verify current scope, impact metrics,
APCs and open-access policy on the publisher's site — these change yearly and must not be stated as fixed.**

---

## A. Leading journals by cluster (with scope notes)

### Construction informatics / digital construction
- **Automation in Construction** (Elsevier) — flagship for digital/automated construction: BIM, robotics,
  computer vision, sensing, AI in construction. Broad and prestigious; high selectivity.
- **Advanced Engineering Informatics** (Elsevier) — informatics, data/knowledge engineering, AI across
  engineering incl. AEC; methodological depth expected.
- **Journal of Computing in Civil Engineering** (ASCE) — computing methods in civil/AEC; algorithmic
  rigor valued.
- **Computer-Aided Civil and Infrastructure Engineering** (Wiley) — AI/optimization/computing for
  infrastructure; very high impact, methods-heavy.
- **Journal of Information Technology in Construction (ITcon)** — open access, IT in construction, no APC;
  strong for openBIM/interoperability work.
- **Developments in the Built Environment** (Elsevier) — open access, broad built-environment scope.

### Construction engineering & management
- **Journal of Construction Engineering and Management** (ASCE) — premier CEM venue: planning, productivity,
  safety, methods, project performance.
- **Construction Management and Economics** (Taylor & Francis) — management, economics, sociology of
  construction; methodological pluralism, strong qualitative tradition.
- **Engineering, Construction and Architectural Management (ECAM)** (Emerald) — management/practice focus.
- **International Journal of Project Management** (Elsevier) — project management broadly (not AEC-only).
- **Journal of Management in Engineering** (ASCE) — engineering management & leadership.
- **International Journal of Construction Management** (Taylor & Francis) — broad CM, more accessible.
- **Construction Innovation** (Emerald) — innovation, process, technology adoption.

### Building performance, energy & environment
- **Energy and Buildings** (Elsevier) — building energy, thermal performance, HVAC, comfort; very large,
  high impact.
- **Building and Environment** (Elsevier) — IEQ, comfort, ventilation, building physics, health.
- **Journal of Building Engineering** (Elsevier) — broad building engineering; high volume.
- **Applied Energy** / **Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews** (Elsevier) — energy systems & reviews
  (RSER is review-focused, very high impact).
- **Building Simulation** (Springer) — simulation methods & applications.
- **Journal of Building Performance Simulation** (Taylor & Francis, IBPSA) — simulation methodology.
- **Building Research & Information** (Taylor & Francis) — policy, performance, sociotechnical; reflective.
- **Sustainable Cities and Society** (Elsevier) — urban sustainability, UBEM, urban heat.
- **Energy & Built Environment** (open access).

### Structural, materials & infrastructure
- **Engineering Structures** (Elsevier), **Structures** (Elsevier), **Journal of Structural Engineering**
  (ASCE), **Structural Health Monitoring** (SAGE), **Construction and Building Materials** (Elsevier),
  **Cement and Concrete Research / Composites** (Elsevier), **Journal of Bridge Engineering** (ASCE).

### Sustainability / LCA
- **Journal of Cleaner Production** (Elsevier), **Building and Environment**, **Resources, Conservation &
  Recycling** (Elsevier), **International Journal of Life Cycle Assessment** (Springer), **Sustainable
  Production and Consumption** (Elsevier).

### Architecture / design / urban
- **Frontiers of Architectural Research** (open access), **Architectural Science Review** (T&F),
  **Environment and Planning B: Urban Analytics and City Science** (SAGE), **Landscape and Urban Planning**
  (Elsevier), **Cities** (Elsevier), **Computers, Environment and Urban Systems** (Elsevier).

### Safety & health
- **Safety Science** (Elsevier), **Accident Analysis & Prevention** (Elsevier), **Journal of Safety
  Research** (Elsevier).

> Society note: **ASCE** journals (American Society of Civil Engineers) are central to US civil/construction
> scholarship; **buildingSMART** and **CIB** anchor openBIM and built-environment research communities;
> **IBPSA** anchors building simulation.

## B. Key conferences (peer-reviewed, archival in many cases)
- **ISARC** — International Symposium on Automation and Robotics in Construction (IAARC). Flagship for
  construction robotics/automation.
- **CIB W78** — Information Technology in Construction.
- **EG-ICE** — European Group for Intelligent Computing in Engineering.
- **ICCCBE** — International Conference on Computing in Civil and Building Engineering.
- **Construction Research Congress (CRC)** — ASCE.
- **eCAADe / CAADRIA / ACADIA / SIGraDi** — computational/architectural design computing.
- **IBPSA Building Simulation** + regional (BS, BSO, SimBuild) — building performance simulation.
- **Lean Construction (IGLC)** — International Group for Lean Construction.
- **CIB World Building Congress**, **Creative Construction Conference**, **EC3 (European Conference on
  Computing in Construction)**.
- **Fabricate**, **RobArch** — robotic fabrication.

## C. Bibliographic databases & search engines
| Source | Strengths | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Scopus** (Elsevier) | Broad, structured metadata, citation analysis, good AEC coverage | Subscription; primary for SLR/bibliometrics |
| **Web of Science** (Clarivate) | Curated Core Collection, JCR impact factors | Subscription; pairs with Scopus for completeness |
| **ASCE Library** | Civil/construction full text | Essential for CEM/structural |
| **ScienceDirect** | Elsevier full text | Many AEC flagships |
| **Engineering Village / Compendex** | Engineering-focused indexing | Good recall for engineering |
| **Google Scholar** | Widest recall, grey literature | Poor precision/metadata; use for snowballing not as sole source |
| **Semantic Scholar / OpenAlex** | Open APIs, citation graphs, TLDRs | Great for programmatic discovery & open bibliometrics |
| **Dimensions** | Grants + pubs + patents linkage | Useful for funding/impact mapping |
| **TRID** | Transportation research | Infrastructure topics |
| **CORE / BASE / DOAJ** | Open-access aggregation | OA discovery |
| **CumInCAD** | Architectural computing proceedings (eCAADe/ACADIA/CAADRIA) | Niche but authoritative |

**Search-source rule for systematic reviews:** use **at least two** major indexed databases (typically
Scopus + Web of Science), justify the choice, and supplement with backward/forward snowballing. Document
exact strings, dates, and counts per source (see the `systematic-review` skill).

## D. Research metrics — use correctly
- **Journal Impact Factor (JIF)** — Clarivate (Web of Science), 2-year citation average. **JCR quartiles
  (Q1–Q4)** within a category are more meaningful than raw JIF across fields.
- **CiteScore** — Scopus equivalent (4-year window).
- **SJR (SCImago Journal Rank)** — prestige-weighted; **SNIP** — field-normalized.
- **h-index** — author/journal productivity+impact; field-dependent, age-biased.
- **Field-Weighted Citation Impact (FWCI)** — normalized (1.0 = world average); good for cross-field
  comparison.
- **Altmetrics** — attention, not quality.

**Cautions:** never compare raw JIF across subfields (energy journals cite faster than management journals);
prefer quartiles/percentiles/FWCI; metrics measure attention, not validity (DORA principles); beware
predatory journals (check DOAJ, COPE membership, Think-Check-Submit).

## E. Open access & costs
- **Gold OA** (APC-funded, immediately open), **Green OA** (self-archived repository/preprint),
  **Diamond/Platinum OA** (no APC — e.g., ITcon), **Hybrid** (subscription journal, optional paid OA).
- **Preprints:** engrXiv, arXiv (some CS/EESS), SSRN; check the target journal's preprint policy first.
- **Mandates:** Plan S / cOAlition S, funder OA requirements — advise authors to check funder and
  institutional policy and available transformative ("read-and-publish") agreements before paying an APC.

## F. Venue-fit heuristic (when recommending journals)
1. Match **topic + method + contribution type** to scope notes above (technical method → AiC/AEI/JCCE;
   management/empirical → JCEM/CME/ECAM; performance/energy → Energy & Buildings/Building & Environment).
2. Check **audience** (practitioner vs methodological vs policy).
3. Weigh **selectivity vs fit vs speed vs OA/APC** and the author's career stage.
4. Always offer a **primary + 2 alternates** spanning ambition levels, and tell the user to verify current
   scope, metrics, APC and turnaround on the publisher site.
